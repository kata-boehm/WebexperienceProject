Greetings, my name is Amasi. The year is 48 BCE. Behind me stands the Great Library of Alexandria — one of the most ambitious centers of knowledge ever built. This place is more than shelves and scrolls; it is what scholars call an epistemic institution A place or system that helps us create, preserve, and share knowledge — like libraries, schools, or even oral traditions. — a place devoted to gathering, preserving, and sharing the truths of our world. Here, knowledge is not hoarded for the powerful, but preserved for all who seek to learn. That is why I’ve chosen to apprentice here — to study, to listen, and to protect the stories that shape who we are. Would you like to join me on my quest for wisdom?
